macOS menu-bar app · UI/UX mockups

Look up a word.
VocabLook remembers it for you.

You keep using the native Ctrl + Cmd + D lookup. VocabLook quietly logs every word and the sentence you found it in, then serves it back as a spaced-repetition flashcard the next day.

01

Capture

Select text anywhere, press Ctrl+Cmd+D. The system Look Up still opens — we just listen.

02

Enrich

We grab the word, its IPA & definition, the surrounding sentence, and the source app.

03

Schedule

Each word becomes a flashcard, first due tomorrow, then spaced by the SM-2 algorithm.

04

Review

A daily nudge opens the deck. Grade each card; the schedule adapts to your memory.

One quick permission

To read the word you look up, VocabLook needs macOS Accessibility access. It never reads anything until you press Ctrl+Cmd+D.

  1. 1
    Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility
  2. 2
    Toggle on VocabLook
  3. 3
    That's it — look up a word to test it
